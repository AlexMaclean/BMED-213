The Design

While Triple Quadrupole Mass Spectrometers are complex and the designs very depending on which model and scientific instrument vendor they are made by, they can generally be said to consist of five main parts.

0 Ionization Chromatographic Column

In the first stage of the process, peptides which have been created by breaking apart the proteins in a sample with protease are ionized, giving them charge and then enter a chromatographic column. The chromatographic column is designed such that molecules elude off of it over time based on some known property, such a hydrophobicity.

1 First Quadrupole

As the name implies, each quadrupole consists of 4 metal rods. These rods are charged such that peptides of a certain mass to charge ratio (m/z) are pushed to the center and along to the next stage while other peptides are pushed out of the quadrupole

2 Collision Cell

In the collision cell the peptides of the right mass to charge ratio to make it past the first quadrupole are further broken apart by gas colliding with them. This process can predictably generate new peptide fragments with different mass to charge ratios

3 Third Quadrupole

The third quadrupole is similar to the second, however it is generally set to a different change. This way the only peptides that make it through the process are those that have the correct mass to charge ratio and break into fragments that also have the correct mass to charge ratio.

4 Detector

At the end of a mass spectrometer there is a detector which simply measures the rate of particles colliding with it. If the machine is working correctly these are only particles which have made it through all three stages.
